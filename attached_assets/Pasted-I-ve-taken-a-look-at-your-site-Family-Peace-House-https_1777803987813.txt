I've taken a look at your site—[Family Peace House](https://peace-house-gateway--uniqueadhikari.replit.app/site.html). Since you've confirmed there are no console errors and private mode didn't help, the issue is likely a **CSS/Layout conflict** or a **Targeting error** in your JavaScript.

Based on the visual layout of your site, here are the three most likely culprits:

### 1. The "Invisible Wall" (Z-Index)
Your AI Assistant button is positioned in a fixed spot on the right. If the main content container or the "Guest Reviews" section has a high `z-index` or a `transform` property (which creates a new stacking context), it might be "covering" the button even if the button looks like it's on top.
* **The Fix:** Force the button to the absolute front.
    ```css
    /* Add this to your assistant button class */
    .ai-assistant-btn {
        z-index: 99999 !important;
        position: fixed !important;
    }
    ```

### 2. ID/Class Mismatch
Check your `script.js` (or wherever your logic lives). If you are using `document.getElementById('ai-btn')` but the button in `site.html` actually has `class="ai-btn"`, the listener will never attach.
* **The Check:** Look at the HTML for the button: 
    `<button id="assistant-trigger">✦ AI Assistant</button>`
* **The JS:** Ensure it matches exactly: 
    `document.querySelector('#assistant-trigger').addEventListener(...)`

### 3. "Dead Zone" from the Replit Overlay
Replit sometimes injects a small "Badge" or an invisible feedback layer in the bottom corners of the preview window. 
* **The Test:** In your CSS, temporarily change the position of the button to the top:
    `top: 20px; right: 20px;`
* If it works at the top but not at the bottom, something in the Replit environment (or a global footer container) is intercepting the click.

### 4. The Event "Bubbling" Issue
If the button is inside a container that also has a click event (like a slider or a link), the click might be getting "hijacked" or cancelled.
* **The Fix:** Try adding `event.stopPropagation()` to your click handler:
    ```javascript
    const btn = document.querySelector('.your-button-class');
    btn.onclick = (e) => {
        e.preventDefault();
        e.stopPropagation();
        console.log("I am finally opening!");
        // open panel code
    };
    ```

**Pro Tip:** Try clicking the **very edge** of the button. If it works on the edge but not the center, you definitely have an invisible element sitting on top of it.
